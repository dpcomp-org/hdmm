About

The PSynDB data generator was designed and built by:

  • Zhiqi Huang, UMass Amherst
  • Ryan McKenna, UMass Amherst
  • George Bissias, UMass Amherst
  • Gerome Miklau, UMass Amherst
  • Michael Hay, Colgate University
  • Ashwin Machanavajjhala, Duke University

This website is the basis of a demonstration presented at VLDB 2019. There is a short paper describing the demo and a poster accompanying the demo.

PSynDB relies on the following recent privacy technologies:

  • The program that generates synthetic data runs on Ektelo, a programming framework for diï¬€erentially private algorithms:

    • Ektelo: A Framework for Defining Differentially-Private Computations
    Dan Zhang, Ryan McKenna, Ios Kotsogiannis, Michael Hay, Ashwin Machanavajjhala, Gerome Miklau
    Conference on Management of Data (SIGMOD) 2018

  • The High-Dimensional Matrix Mechanism (HDMM) is used to adapt to the workload specified by the user:

    • Optimizing Error of High-Dimensional Statistical Queries Under Differential Privacy
    Ryan McKenna, Gerome Miklau, Michael Hay, Ashwin Machanavajjhala
    Proceedings of the VLDB (PVLDB) 2018

  • Lastly, graphical-model based estimation is used to enforce consistency, combine the noisy measurements, and sample synthetic tuples:

    • Graphical-model based estimation and inference for differential privacy
    Ryan McKenna, Dan Sheldon, Gerome Miklau
    International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML) 2019
